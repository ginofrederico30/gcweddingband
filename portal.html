2. SCOPE OF SERVICES

  1. Artist shall provide all musical equipment, amplifiers, microphone and all other equipment necessary for performance and services listed below:
    1. 8-Piece Reception Band
    2. Sound & Lighting Production
    3. Live Ceremony Music
    4. Jazz Cocktail Band
    5. MC for Reception
    6. DJ Services
  2. Artist shall provide services for the entire duration of the event specified in Section 2a.
  3. Artist is entitled to one meal provided by the Client, which must be equivalent to what is served to guests. Meals are required for all team members, which consist of 10 individuals (8 band members and 2 sound technicians).
  4. The 8-piece reception band performance is scheduled for a 3-hour block, which includes 2.5 hours of live music and one 30 minute break. The timing of the break will be at the Artist's discretion. During this break, the Artist will provide DJ services to maintain continuous entertainment.
  5. First dance and special dances (e.g., parent dances) are DJ'd and not included in the live band performance. If the Client would like the band to perform one or more of these special dances live, Artist may accommodate the request for an additional charge.

3. CLIENT REQUIREMENTS

  1. Client shall ensure the availability of sufficient power for the performance. The Artist's equipment requires standard 3-prong grounded outlets and does not exceed the power demands of typical outlets. For optimal performance and to minimize the risk of overloading circuits, the artist recommends the provision of two separate circuits, each equipped with grounded outlets, located within 25 feet of the performance area.
  2. Client shall provide a performance area measuring 24' × 15', with 3' of clearance on each side for speaker placement, totaling a 27' × 18' footprint. Additionally, there must be a minimum of 33 inches of clearance in front of the performance area for subwoofer placement. A stage is not required, as the performance area can be on the floor or on a stage. Please note, we do not provide stages; stages may be supplied by the venue or rented from a third party if desired. These space requirements are intended to ensure optimal sound quality and performance setup. If these dimensions cannot be fully accommodated, please let us know in advance. We're happy to work with you and adjust as needed.
  3. If the performance is outdoors, the Client must provide adequate coverage for the stage area, such as a deep, dry overhang or a tent with sides, to protect the Artist and equipment from inclement weather. Additionally, the staging area must be dry, level, and stable. Performances cannot take place on wet grass, mud, uneven ground, or any other surface that poses a risk to equipment or performer safety (e.g., unstable tripods or speaker stands). If these conditions are not met, the Artist may stop the performance at their discretion, secure equipment, and resume once conditions improve, provided the Client requests it and it does not conflict with another booking. The Client will be liable for any equipment damage caused by the lack of proper cover or an unsuitable staging surface.

6. GOOD COMPANY PLANNER (GCP)

The Good Company Planner (also referred to as GCP) is a collection of forms provided by the Artist to the Client after contract completion. It collects live music preferences and wedding details (schedule, wedding party names, dinner playlists, etc.)

  1. Client will fill out the GCP to the best of their ability.
  2. GCP is to be completed by the Client 1 month prior to the wedding date. If more time is needed, Client will notify the Artist.
  3. Client will provide playlists for key moments (e.g., dinner music, wedding party entrance, etc.). Artist prefers receiving playlists via Spotify but can accommodate other formats if needed.
  4. Artist reserves the right to charge additional fees based on logistical or technical information provided in the GCP. For example, if the ceremony takes place in a separate room or at a significant distance from the reception, requiring major equipment relocation, additional staffing, or expanded setup, extra charges may apply.

7. SONG REQUESTS

  1. Artist will make best effort to perform songs requested via the GCP.
  2. All songs requested for live performance are for consideration by the Artist, but are not guaranteed.
  3. Artist will not take requests during live performance.
  4. Artist will not play or perform songs marked 'Do Not Play' in the GCP.
  5. Client may request songs to be played via Spotify or other pre-recorded formats at any time before or during moments when pre-recorded music is played.

8. ATTIRE

  1. Client will provide dress code details below: You fill Dress code selected:
  2. Client may request attire until 7 days before the event, including standard dress code terms. For rental or special orders, Client must allow time for the Artist to acquire the attire. Client agrees to reimburse the Artist for unavoidable attire expenses over $50, such as tuxedo rentals or color-specific purchases. Expenses will only be incurred if necessary to fulfill the Client's request, and the Artist will first attempt to meet the request without additional costs.

9. PERSONAL SERVICE GUARANTEE

  1. Artist will serve as your exclusive event entertainer. In the unlikely event of circumstances beyond our control (hospitalization, accident, etc), Artist will make every effort to find a replacement for your event.
  2. Artist will provide service as described in Section 2ai, with 8 band members present. In the unlikely event that an individual band member cannot perform, Artist will be responsible for finding a replacement for all responsibilities of the missing band member.
  3. Artist will provide service as described in Section 2aii, with 3 band members present. In the unlikely event that an individual band member cannot perform, Artist will be responsible for finding a replacement for all responsibilities of the missing band member.

10. ACCESS AND SETUP

  1. The Artist is not responsible for delays due to misdirection.
  2. The Client is responsible for providing the Artist with access to the event site for setup, as well as notifying the Artist of any changes in time or location.
  3. The Artist requires at least 2 hours before the event start time for soundcheck and setup.
  4. The Artist requires at least 1 hour after the event for breakdown and loadout.

11. ATTENDANCE

  1. Artist attendance is guaranteed in compliance with the terms of this contract. In the highly unlikely event of a 'no-show' to a regularly booked, scheduled, and fully paid-for event, Artist will refund the balance, in full, to the Contract Executor/Signee.

14. ACTS OF GOD

  2. In the event of extreme weather or an "Act of God" requiring evacuation, the Artist will attempt to reschedule once the evacuation is lifted. The balance paid will serve as a retainer for the new date, with one year to redeem the credit. If not redeemed within the year, the Artist will keep the payment.
